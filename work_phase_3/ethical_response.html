Internal Memo: Ethical Review of Newsletter Subscription Plugin

To: Supervising Manager, Connect Communities Initiative (CCI)

From: Tianhao Chen, Design Team

Date: May 12, 2025

Subject: Ethical Review and Recommendations for the Funder-Mandated Newsletter Subscription Plugin

1. Context Identification & Client Goal

This memo addresses the client brief's requirement to integrate a specific third-party newsletter subscription plugin provided by a funder for the "Global Roots Community" website. The stated goal from CCI is to "better understand its audience" to enhance service delivery and community engagement.

The plugin, as specified, requests an extensive list of user data points beyond a typical newsletter subscription:

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Occupation
  • Annual income
  • Household size
  • Sexuality
  • Gender
  • Spending habits
  • Type of phone they currently use
  • Monthly spend on dining out/takeaway food
  • Number of credit cards they have with the credit limit on each card

This level of data collection forms the basis of our ethical review, considering stakeholders including users, CCI, the funder, and the broader community.

2. Ethical Analysis based on ACS Framework

Our analysis, guided by the Australian Computing Society (ACS) Code of Professional Ethics, identifies several critical ethical concerns arising from the plugin's data requirements. These points are drawn directly from our A2 Design Research findings:

2.1. Potential Harms Identified:

  • Severe Privacy Intrusion: Requesting intimate details (sexuality, income, credit card limits) far exceeds necessity for a newsletter and is deeply invasive, especially for newcomers who are the target audience.
  • Risk of Misuse & Discrimination: Such detailed data, if misused by the funder or through a breach, could lead to profiling, predatory marketing, or discrimination.
  • Heightened Security Risks: Storing this volume of sensitive personal and financial data creates a high-value target for malicious actors, increasing the risk of data breaches.
  • Erosion of Trust: Excessive data collection can damage the trust CCI aims to build with international communities, potentially harming its reputation and mission.
  • Lack of Genuine Informed Consent: Users, particularly those new to the country or with language barriers, may not fully grasp the implications of providing such extensive data, questioning the genuineness of their consent.
  • Exclusion & Chilling Effects: Users uncomfortable with these data demands may opt out, hindering CCI's inclusivity goals and reducing newsletter reach.

2.2. Alignment with ACS Code of Professional Ethics:

The plugin's requirements conflict with several ACS core values and principles:

  • The Primacy of the Public Interest: Mandates placing public interest (user privacy, security) above sectional interests (funder's data desires). The plugin appears to prioritize funder interests.
  • The Enhancement of Quality of Life: The potential harms (stress, risk of misuse, erosion of trust) detract from user well-being rather than enhancing it.
  • Honesty & Trustworthiness: Requesting such extensive data for a "newsletter" could be perceived as dishonest, eroding trustworthiness.
  • Respect: Collecting intimate details without compelling, user-centric justification demonstrates a lack of respect for user autonomy and privacy.
  • Minimise Harm (Principle): The plugin, as described, maximizes potential harm rather than minimizing it.

3. Recommendations & Proposed Solutions

Based on the ethical analysis, and aligning with the problem-solving approach from A2, we propose the following tiered recommendations for addressing the funder's newsletter plugin requirement:

The most ethical and user-respecting approach is to collect only the absolute minimum data necessary for a newsletter: the user's email address.

  • Action A: Configure Existing Plugin: Investigate if the funder's provided plugin can be technically configured or modified to *only* request and store the email address field, disabling all other data collection points.
  • Action B: Utilise Alternative, Privacy-Focused Plugin: If the funder's plugin cannot be restricted, we strongly recommend using a standard, reputable newsletter service (e.g., Mailchimp, Sendinblue, or a simple, custom-built solution) that focuses solely on email collection and adheres to privacy best practices.

Justification: This solution directly upholds user privacy, significantly minimises security and misuse risks, fosters trust, and aligns with ACS ethical principles. Understanding the audience can be achieved through less intrusive, voluntary methods such as anonymised surveys, community forum feedback (if applicable to the site's features), or aggregated, non-identifiable website analytics, once the community is established.

If, due to unavoidable funding constraints, CCI must proceed with a plugin that requests more than an email address, the following safeguards are non-negotiable to mitigate ethical harms:

  • Radical Transparency: Before any data entry, users must be very clearly, simply, and prominently informed about:
    • Exactly what data is being collected.
    • Precisely how the funder (and any other party) will use this data.
    • Who will have access to the data.
    • Data storage duration and security measures.
    • The user's rights regarding their data (access, correction, deletion).
  • Absolute Optionality: All data fields, with the sole exception of the email address, MUST be strictly optional. Users must be able to subscribe to the newsletter by providing only their email. No adverse consequences or reduced functionality should result from not providing optional data.
  • Refuse Collection of Highly Sensitive Data: CCI must refuse to collect the most sensitive data categories, specifically:
    • Sexuality
    • Detailed financial data (annual income, spending habits, monthly dining spend, number of credit cards and their limits)
    The collection of "Occupation" and "Household size" should also be strongly questioned for its necessity for a newsletter.
  • Phased & Contextual Collection (Alternative for Non-Sensitive Demographics): If some general, non-sensitive demographic data (e.g., broad age group, country of origin if relevant to CCI's tailored content) is deemed essential by the funder, this should NOT be part of the initial newsletter signup. Instead, it could be requested *optionally* at a later stage, for example, within a user's profile settings if they choose to create one, with a very clear explanation of how this specific information will directly benefit them (e.g., "Help us tailor content for your region").

Justification: This compromise attempts to mitigate harm by empowering users with clear information and genuine choice, though significant ethical and trust risks remain compared to Option 1. It requires robust oversight and a commitment to user well-being above funder data requests.

4. Draft Response to Client/Funder (for your review)

Below is a proposed draft communication to the client/funder regarding the newsletter plugin. This draft is based on our strong recommendation for Option 1 (Data Minimisation).

Subject: Discussion Regarding the Newsletter Subscription Plugin for Global Roots Community

Dear [Client/Funder Contact Person],

Thank you for providing the newsletter subscription plugin for the Global Roots Community website. We appreciate the goal of wanting to better understand the platform's audience to enhance future service delivery.

In our commitment to building a trustworthy and inclusive platform for international communities, and adhering to professional ethical standards (including the ACS Code of Professional Ethics), we've conducted a review of the data points requested by the current plugin. We've identified significant concerns regarding the extent of personal and financial information requested, such as [mention 2-3 most sensitive examples like sexuality, detailed financial data], which go well beyond what is typically required or expected for a newsletter subscription.

Collecting such extensive sensitive data poses potential risks to user privacy, data security, and could inadvertently erode the trust we are working hard to establish with newcomers to Australia. Our primary aim is to create a welcoming and safe environment, and we believe that a more privacy-respecting approach to the newsletter subscription will better serve this core mission and ultimately benefit both the users and the long-term success of the Global Roots Community.

Therefore, we would like to propose focusing the newsletter subscription on collecting only the essential information: the user's email address. This approach aligns with best practices for data minimisation and user respect. We are confident that audience understanding can still be effectively achieved through alternative, less intrusive methods as the community grows, such as voluntary, anonymised feedback surveys or by observing engagement with content relevant to their needs.

We are keen to discuss how we can either configure the provided plugin to meet this data minimisation standard or explore alternative tools that are specifically designed for privacy-conscious newsletter management. We believe this collaborative approach will ensure the Global Roots Community platform is both effective in its outreach and exemplary in its respect for user trust and ethical data handling.

We look forward to discussing this further with you at your earliest convenience.

Sincerely,

The Connect Communities Initiative Design Team (via Tianhao Chen)

5. Conclusion & Next Steps for CCI

The design team strongly recommends that CCI advocate for the Preferred Solution (Option 1) with the funder – drastically minimizing data collection for the newsletter to email addresses only. This is the most ethically sound path to protect users, uphold CCI's integrity, and foster a truly welcoming "Global Roots Community."

If Option 1 proves unachievable, the Compromise Solution (Option 2) with its strict mitigations (transparency, optionality, refusal of most sensitive data) should be pursued, with full awareness of the remaining risks.

We are prepared to discuss these recommendations further, provide technical advice on plugin configuration or alternatives, and assist in any communications regarding these critical ethical considerations. Our shared goal is a platform that is not only functional and engaging but fundamentally ethical and user-respecting.
